The Concept

A jukebox
in orbit.

When Shinichiro Watanabe and composer Yoko Kanno built Cowboy Bebop, they wired its skeleton out of 20th-century music. The episodes aren't called “Episode 1, 2, 3” — they're Sessions, the word for a jazz recording date.

And the titles? They're a record-collection in disguise. Bill Evans' Waltz for Debby becomes Waltz for Venus. The Rolling Stones, Queen, Led Zeppelin, KISS, Herbie Hancock and the Chess blues catalogue all leave fingerprints. Some titles name a song outright; others summon a whole genre — blues, funk, samba, boogie-woogie.

This page breaks down all twenty-six. For each, the song it points to, the era it comes from, and why Watanabe chose it — how the story, mood and Kanno's score pay tribute.
